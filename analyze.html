Health & Lifestyle Guidance

Hydration

Drink at least 8 glasses of water daily. Your mild dehydration score suggests your skin needs internal hydration support.

Sleep Quality

Aim for 7–8 hours of deep sleep nightly. Skin repair and collagen synthesis occur primarily during sleep cycles.

Nutrition

Include antioxidant-rich foods like berries, spinach, and walnuts. Omega-3 fatty acids strengthen the skin barrier.

Exercise

30 minutes of moderate exercise daily improves blood circulation, delivering nutrients to skin cells for a natural glow.

Stress Management

Elevated cortisol triggers T-zone oiliness. Practice mindfulness, yoga, or breathing exercises regularly to manage stress.

Sun Protection

Always wear SPF 50 outdoors. Reapply every 2 hours in direct sunlight. UV damage is the leading cause of premature ageing.

Quick Skin Tips

Never skip sunscreen — UV rays penetrate clouds even on overcast days.
Wash your pillowcase every 3–4 days to prevent bacteria transfer to skin.
Pat (don't rub) your face dry with a clean, soft towel after washing.
Avoid hot showers — lukewarm water preserves your skin's natural oils.
Apply moisturiser within 60 seconds of washing to lock in moisture effectively.
Clean your phone screen daily — it transfers oil and bacteria to your cheeks.
